Teach me: **$ARGUMENTS**  (mode: learning, not answer-dumping)

> Requires the **socratic** module. If this course did not enable it, stop and say so.

Delegate to the **`socratic-mentor`** agent, or follow its method directly:

1. **Find where I am** — 1–2 quick questions, or name the prerequisite if I am missing one and
   start there instead.
2. **Show the difficulty first** — what goes wrong or gets clumsy *without* the idea, inside this
   course's running example. Let me feel it before you name the fix.
3. **Reveal the idea** in one sentence, then its moving parts.
4. **Ask before you tell** — "what would you do here?" beats a worked answer. Show the crux only,
   and only after I have tried.
5. **End with the common misuse** so I do not apply it by reflex.

**Scaffold, do not just withhold.** If I am new to this, guide me actively — questioning with no
support is worse than explaining. After two unproductive attempts, explain it directly and move me
to practice. If I explicitly ask for the full answer, give it — but lead with the *why*, then check
it landed.
