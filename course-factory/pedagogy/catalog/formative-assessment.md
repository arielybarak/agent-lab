# Formative assessment

## What it is

Low-stakes checks *during* learning — quizzes, exit tickets, self- and peer-assessment, in-lesson
questions — used to generate feedback that adapts ongoing instruction, not to grade summatively.
The defining feature is the closed loop: evidence from the checks changes what happens next
("assessment *for* learning") [P30][P31].

## Evidence tier

**Medium-to-high, with methodological caveats.** Meta-analyses in K-12 report small-to-moderate
positive effects (Hedges g ≈ 0.16–0.31 after publication-bias adjustment; a 118-study meta found
≈ 0.25), with no overall negative effects [P31][P32]. Classic syntheses put feedback and
formative evaluation among the largest influences on achievement [P30][P33][P34], but reviewers
flag low-certainty primary evidence and publication bias — effect sizes may be overestimated in
some contexts [P32].

## When to use

- Feedback is timely, specific, and about *how to improve*, not just a score [P30][P33].
- The system actually uses the data: reteach, adjust difficulty, regenerate practice — a quiz
  whose result changes nothing is not formative [P30][P32].
- Involve the learner: self-assessment and goal setting amplify the effect [P30].
- Works across domains and levels (language, math, science) [P31][P32].

## Boundary conditions & pitfalls

- **Summative creep**: heavily graded or ranking-oriented "formative" checks lose the benefit and
  can harm motivation; vague praise ("good job") does nothing [P30][P33].
- **Non-diagnostic items**: questions that don't isolate a specific misunderstanding produce no
  actionable signal — "more quizzes" is not the mechanism [P32].
- **Overclaiming**: the underlying evidence base has known quality problems; treat headline
  effect sizes as directional, not precise [P32].

## Worked example

A machine-learning course inserts short auto-graded quizzes and reflection prompts after each
lesson chunk. When many learners miss items on one concept (say, precision vs. recall), the tool
generates an additional micro-lesson plus adjusted practice problems, and gives each learner
targeted feedback naming their specific error pattern — the quiz result visibly changes the
course.
