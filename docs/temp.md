
Essentials of Team Collaboration

1) Setting the stage for strong Team Collaboration
1.1) Six key questions for effective collaboration
The six basic questions teams should answer to build a strong foundation for collaboration are: Who, What, Why, Where, When, and How. Answering these helps your team define key parameters, set boundaries, clarify expectations, and identify potential disagreements early, ensuring everyone is aligned before starting the work. 

1.2) Six key questions for effective collaboration
When assessing your team, consider asking questions like:

Do team members have the right skills and experience?
What is each member's authority level, and do you have a decision maker?
Are team members available as needed?
Does the team have the necessary diversity?
Do members have complementary work styles and good interpersonal relationships?

These questions help you identify strengths and gaps early, setting the stage for strong collaboration and avoiding potential conflicts

1.3) Clarify the team's goal and purpose
Here are the key takeaways from the video:

Clarity on your team's goals (the what) and mission (the why) is essential to avoid confusion and dysfunction.
Different team members often have varying understandings, so it's important to surface and align these early.
A simple technique is to have each person write down their view of the goals and mission, then discuss similarities and differences to clarify scope and expectations.

This approach helps your team stay focused and prevents costly miscommunications or conflicts down the road.

1.4) Clarify constraints, expectations, and processes up front
Discuss and agree early on where the team will work and meet, considering factors like remote work and location challenges.
Clarify when the team will work, including working hours, time zones, meeting frequency, and deadlines to keep everyone aligned.
Define how the team will work together by establishing clear communication methods and processes to avoid misunderstandings and inefficiencies.

These steps help prevent common collaboration issues and set your team up for success.

2) Five tactics to encourage healthy Team Collaboration
2.1) Develop team ground rules
Start by explaining why ground rules are important to ensure everyone understands their value in effective collaboration.
Have each team member reflect on past team experiences and suggest one ground rule on sticky notes.
Review all suggestions together and discuss them openly to capture the team's personality and needs.
Vote on the proposed ground rules and only adopt those that everyone supports, ensuring a bottom-up approach for better commitment.

This process helps create clear, agreed-upon guidelines that improve teamwork and prevent dysfunction.

2.2) Build relationships early and often
Relationships significantly impact how well team members work together, making collaboration smoother and conflict easier to resolve.
Building relationships should be an ongoing, incremental process integrated into everyday team activities, not just occasional events.
Simple practices like sharing personal stories during meetings or informal gatherings (like "Bagel Thursdays") help break down barriers and strengthen connections regularly.

2.3) Identify team members' preferred communication mode
People have preferred communication modes (face-to-face, phone, email, text), and using their preferred mode leads to quicker and fuller responses.
Simply asking team members about their preferred communication style and pet peeves can significantly improve how the team works together.
The "four walls communication activity" is a practical way to identify these preferences and foster understanding, which enhances both task completion and team cohesion.

2.4) Tips for great online collaboration
Choose collaboration tools thoughtfully to enhance your team's effectiveness without overwhelming them.
Important tools include content management systems for sharing and managing information, calendaring systems for scheduling, polling tools for gathering input, and video conferencing for communication.
Remember, tools support collaboration but don't replace the need for the team to actively work together and communicate effectively.

2.5) Take periodic temperature checks
Regularly review how the team is working together to identify successes and areas for improvement.
Use debriefing or "temperature check" sessions where each team member shares what’s working well and what could be done differently.
These check-ins help teams adapt to changes like new members, shifting projects, or updated processes, fostering continuous improvement and stronger collaboration.

3) Conclusion

3.1) Develop best practices to enhance Team Collaboration
Effective collaboration requires deliberate effort, including meetings to clarify the team's purpose, goals, roles, and processes upfront.
Developing team ground rules that reflect the team's unique work styles and personalities helps set clear expectations and prevent misunderstandings.
Building relationships continuously within the team fosters trust and discourages cliques.
Understanding and respecting each member's communication preferences is crucial for smooth collaboration.
